Hall Life

Container
1. Can I check-in after office hours?
Text Area

Check-in/ -out related

In general, hall offices operate from 8:00 am to 10:00 pm daily. If you plan to arrive and check-in outside of office hours, please email the individual Hall Office at least 2 days in advance to make special arrangements. If you have any questions or need to change your check-in time, you can contact your Hall Office directly. 

2. Can my parents or friends enter my hall to help me to check-in?
Text Area

Check-in/ -out related

Residents are allowed to host up to two visitors within the visitor hours (9:00 a.m. - 11:00 p.m.).  When your visitors arrive, please ensure that you register with them at the Hall Office counter. For detailed information on visitor regulations, please refer to the hall rules.

3. How can I deal with the humid weather in Hong Kong?
Text Area

Living tips

We understand that high humidity can be uncomfortable. To assist you in coping with the humidity, simply visit the Hall Office where there are dehumidifiers available for borrowing. 

4. I have some unwanted clothes. Is there a place at HKUST that I can donate such clothes to?
Text Area

Green living

There are used clothes recycling bins at UG Hall I, UG Hall VI, and the Lo Ka Chung University Center next to the University Apartments Tower A. Please make sure the items you donate are clean, while used underwear and socks are not suitable. 

5. Is there a quiet place that I can pray at within the hall community?
Text Area

Hall facility

The Reflection Room at UG Hall IX is for prayer and reflection for all faiths. It opens 24 hours between 29 August 2024 to 3 June 2025. Hall residents who wish to use the reflection room are required to complete a registration form here and email to ughix@ust.hk. You will receive an email confirmation in 3 working days.

Hall/ Room Swapping

Container
6. I want to swap hall/ room, what should I do?
Text Area

In each residential term (Fall and Spring), there will be an official swapping period which is usually 2 weeks after the official check-in period. Two residents who wish to swap should complete the Hall/ Room Swapping Application Form online. Approved swap cases will be notified via email.

 

For your swapping request to be justifiable, please note the swap should fulfil the following:
•    a same gender room type,  
•    consents from all roommate(s) is/are obtained (if any), and
•    the request is approved by the respective Residence Master(s) and Hall Office(s).

7. When can I apply for swapping?
Text Area

Swapping period is usually conducted during the check-in period, announcement will be made to eligible residents and on the SHRLO Website. You are encouraged to observe the application deadline in advance and submit your application on time. NO late submission is allowed.

8. I accepted a hall offer, can I delay check-in procedure until approval of my hall/ room swapping request?
Text Area

No, only current residents are allowed to swap their bedspaces, which means you must first become a resident by checking in then to participate in swapping. You may consider completing check-in procedure without moving in to minimize the hassle.

9. Can I swap with residents in different halls?
Text Area

Yes, inter-hall swapping is allowed. However, new students are NOT allowed to swap to Jockey Club Hall. Exchange students can only swap to the SAME room type. And you are responsible for the hall fee difference if you swap to a room type with higher rate.

10. There is an empty bed in my friend's room. Can I swap to that empty bedspace?
Text Area

No, only swapping between occupied bedspaces is allowed. The current empty bedspaces are intended to be available for future topping up exercises (i.e. allocation for waitlisted applicants) due to overwhelming hall-place demand. 

 

Besides, an empty bed does not necessarily mean that it is not assigned to anyone. Please contact Hall Office to confirm whether the bedspace is occupied or not.

13. If I cannot find my roommate and get his/her consent, can I still submit a swap request?
Text Area

Please contact your Hall office to confirm whether your roommate’s bedspace is assigned or not. If the bedspace is not yet assigned, you do not need to get the consent. But if the bedspace is assigned, you are supposed to find your own way to contact your roommate. SHRLO may not be able to help with this due to data privacy issues. Any late submissions will not be considered. You are thus recommended to raise any concerns before the swapping application deadline.

11. Can I swap with a student who withdraws hall residence?
Text Area

No, you cannot. The resident’s bedspace will return to the central pool of bedspace after hall withdrawal.

12. Can I swap for a part of my residential period? (e.g. only Fall Term or Spring Term)
Text Area

No, you cannot. A swapping shall apply to the rest of your offered residential period.

13. Can I submit more than one swapping request?
Text Area

You are allowed to swap more than once within swapping period. But you can only submit one swap request each time. No new request can be submitted if there is a pending request or your approved swapping is yet to complete by relocation.

14. What should I do if I cannot find a swap partner?
Text Area

SHRLO would not help with matching swap partners. You need to find one by yourself.

15. How can I apply for swapping?
Text Area

Click “Submit Swapping Request” of https://studenthousing.hkust.edu.hk/page/ApplySwap

16. Why can't I submit my swap request?
Text Area

You may have pending requests that need your response. Click “Accept/Reject Swap" of https://studenthousing.hkust.edu.hk/page/ApplySwap

17. Where can I check the response status/result?
Text Area

Click “Check Swapping Application Status” of https://studenthousing.hkust.edu.hk/page/ApplySwap

You will receive an automated email after the swap request is approved.

18. When will I receive approval for my swap request?
Text Area

A swapping request will be processed when ALL stakeholders, including requester’s roommate(s), swapping partner, swapping partner(s)’ roommate(s) respond to provide their consent. After that, the request will ONLY be approved upon endorsement of Hall Office(s) and approval by Residence Master(s) (applicable to inter-hall swapping).

 

You will receive an automated email when the swap request is approved and subsequent adjustment (e.g. residential period, hall fee, etc.) is updated in system.

19. What if my roommate/swap partner does not respond to my request?
Text Area

SHRLO will not help you to contact residents involved. You can find their contact details in “Checking Swapping Application Status” of https://studenthousing.hkust.edu.hk/page/ApplySwap

20. Can my swapping partner, my roommate(s) or my swapping partner’s roommate(s) accept my swap request after the application deadline?
Text Area

A swap request can only be processed when ALL stakeholders, including the requester’s roommate(s), the swapping partner, and the roommate(s) of the swapping partner have responded to provide their consent before the application deadline. Late responses from any of the involved residents will not be considered. You are advised to contact all stakeholders regarding your swap request prior to the application deadline to avoid any delays in response.

21. What if stipulated relocation dates are not feasible for me?
Text Area

Three relocation date options are provided in swap request application for your preference. You can indicate your need of alternative arrangement in remarks for our consideration. If you cannot relocate as instructed by the approval email, you need to contact respective Hall Office(s) for further handling.

22. I paid the hall fee for my original room, then swapped to a different room type in another hall; why is it shown on my SIS records that I did not settle my hall fee?
Text Area

Once your swapping request is approved, paid hall fee for your original room will be reversed to your SIS account. Hall fee instalment will be re-posted due to adjustment of residence at different bedspaces. If you have other outstanding charges (e.g. tuition fee) at your SIS account by the time, the reversed hall fee may be automatically absorbed by those outstanding charges (instead of your hall fees) under the system setting. Therefore, the hall fee of your new room will remain outstanding at your SIS account. In such, you are advised to settle any overdue amount in due course.

 

Even if you do not have any other outstanding fee at your SIS account, there may be a hall fee difference that you are required to settle if the hall fee for your new room being higher than your original one after the swapping.
